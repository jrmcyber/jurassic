Jurassic Park

Isla Nublar · 1993

Director: Steven Spielberg Runtime: 127 min Rating: PG-13

Official Trailer

Synopsis

Billionaire John Hammond has created a theme park on Isla Nublar populated with cloned dinosaurs. Before opening, he invites paleontologist Dr. Alan Grant, paleobotanist Dr. Ellie Sattler, and mathematician Dr. Ian Malcolm to certify the park's safety. When a corrupt employee deactivates the security systems, the dinosaurs break free. The group must survive against creatures that haven't walked the Earth for 65 million years — including the terrifying Tyrannosaurus Rex and intelligent Velociraptors.

The film explores themes of chaos theory, genetic ethics, and humanity's hubris in trying to control nature — themes that defined the entire trilogy and changed cinema forever.

Behind the Scenes & Trivia

The T-Rex roar was created from a baby elephant's squeal (slowed down), an alligator's gurgle, and a tiger's snarl. The sound design became one of cinema's most recognizable effects.

The film used both CGI and animatronics. The T-Rex animatronic weighed 9 tons and was 40 feet long. Only 4 minutes of screen time featured CGI dinosaurs — the rest were practical effects.

The famous "water ripple in the cup" scene was made by pulling a guitar string under the car. It took multiple takes to get the timing right with the approaching T-Rex footsteps.

Jurassic Park became the highest-grossing film of all time upon release (until Titanic in 1997). It won three Academy Awards for Best Visual Effects, Best Sound Editing, and Best Sound Mixing.
