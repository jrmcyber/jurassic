The Lost World

Isla Sorna · 1997

Director: Steven Spielberg Runtime: 129 min Rating: PG-13

Official Trailer

Synopsis

Four years after the Jurassic Park disaster, Dr. Ian Malcolm discovers that InGen has been conducting secret operations on Isla Sorna — "Site B" where the dinosaurs were originally bred. A research team is sent to document the creatures, but a rival expedition from InGen arrives with a different mission: capture dinosaurs for a new theme park in San Diego.

As the two groups clash, they must survive against packs of raptors, the aggressive T-Rex family, and other prehistoric dangers. The film culminates in a T-Rex rampage through suburban San Diego — one of cinema's most memorable sequences.

Behind the Scenes & Trivia

The San Diego rampage scene cost $15 million to film. The production built a full-scale suburban street set on a Universal Studios backlot.

The raptor attack in the tall grass became an iconic sequence. The grass was real and had to be maintained throughout filming.

The Lost World was Michael Crichton's sequel novel, released in 1995. Spielberg adapted it while Jurassic Park was still being edited.

The cliff scene with the trailer hanging over the edge used a combination of practical effects and CGI. The actors were in a real trailer suspended by a crane.
