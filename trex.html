Tyrannosaurus Rex

Apex Predator

Field Report

The Tyrannosaurus Rex is the undisputed apex predator of Isla Nublar. Her breakout scene in the rain remains one of cinema's most terrifying moments — the fence, the goat, the silence, then the roar.

She doesn't just hunt. She commands. In the original film, she tears through the park and later saves the group by killing the raptors in the visitor center. In The Lost World, she rampages through San Diego. In Jurassic Park III, she meets her match.

Vital Statistics

Length40 ft (12.3 m)

Lifespan25–30 years
DietCarnivore

Appearances

1993 Jurassic Park — Breaks out of her paddock, attacks the tour vehicles, and kills the raptors in the visitor center.
1997 The Lost World — A mated pair (Buck and Doe) protecting their infant. The Buck rampages through San Diego.
2001 Jurassic Park III — Brief appearance; killed by the Spinosaurus in a shocking fight.
  • Sound Design: The T-Rex roar was created from a baby elephant's squeal (slowed down), an alligator's gurgle, and a tiger's snarl.
  • Animatronic: The T-Rex animatronic weighed 9 tons and was 40 feet long.
  • Water Ripple: The famous "water ripple" scene was achieved by pulling a guitar string under the car.
  • Vision Myth: "Don't move. She can't see us if we don't move." — This is fiction. T-Rex had excellent vision.
  • The T-Rex is the only dinosaur to appear in all three original Jurassic Park films.
  • She is often referred to as "Rexy" by fans and the film crew.
  • The T-Rex breakout scene cost over $1.5 million to film.
  • Her bite force is the strongest of any land animal — ever.
