Film Appearances: Jurassic Park, The Lost World, Jurassic Park III

Length: 40 feet (12.3 meters)

Height: 15-20 feet (4.6-6 meters)

Weight: 7-8 tons

Diet: Carnivore

Facts: The T-Rex roar was created from a baby elephant's squeal, an alligator's gurgle, and a tiger's snarl. The animatronic weighed 9 tons and was 40 feet long.

Film Appearances: Jurassic Park, The Lost World, Jurassic Park III

Length: 15 feet (4.5 meters)

Height: 6 feet (1.8 meters)

Weight: 330 pounds (150 kg)

Diet: Carnivore

Facts: Real velociraptors were turkey-sized and had feathers. The film version was actually based on Deinonychus.

Film Appearances: Jurassic Park III

Length: 50 feet (15.2 meters)

Height: 20 feet (6 meters) at sail

Weight: 12-20 tons

Diet: Carnivore (fish-eater)

Facts: Spinosaurus was larger than T-Rex and famously killed one in Jurassic Park III. Its distinctive sail made it unique.

Film Appearances: Jurassic Park, Jurassic Park III

Length: 85 feet (26 meters)

Height: 40-50 feet (12-15 meters)

Weight: 30-50 tons

Diet: Herbivore

Facts: Brachiosaurus was the first dinosaur seen in Jurassic Park. They could eat up to 1,000 pounds of plants daily.

Film Appearances: Jurassic Park, The Lost World

Length: 26-30 feet (8-9 meters)

Height: 10 feet (3 meters)

Weight: 6-12 tons

Diet: Herbivore

Facts: The Triceratops seen in Jurassic Park was a sick one that Dr. Sattler treated. Its three horns and frill were used for defense.

Film Appearances: Jurassic Park

Length: 10 feet (3 meters)

Height: 4 feet (1.2 meters)

Weight: 200 pounds (90 kg)

Diet: Carnivore

Facts: The frill and venom-spitting abilities were fictional. Real Dilophosaurus did not have these features.

Film Appearances: The Lost World, Jurassic Park III

Length: 3 feet (1 meter)

Height: 1 foot (0.3 meters)

Weight: 5-10 pounds (2-4.5 kg)

Diet: Carnivore

Facts: Compies (as they're nicknamed) hunt in packs. They are among the smallest dinosaurs in the Jurassic Park series.

Film Appearances: Jurassic Park III

Wingspan: 33 feet (10 meters)

Weight: 100 pounds (45 kg)

Diet: Piscivore (fish-eater)

Facts: Pteranodons are not dinosaurs — they're pterosaurs, flying reptiles. Their crests were used for display and steering.
