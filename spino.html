Spinosaurus

Apex Predator

Field Report

The Spinosaurus is the largest carnivorous dinosaur to appear in the Jurassic Park franchise. Its most infamous moment came in Jurassic Park III, when it killed a fully grown T-Rex in a brutal fight — a decision that divided fans worldwide.

Spinosaurus was semi-aquatic, spending much of its time in water hunting fish. Its massive sail made it unique among theropods, and its size made it a true apex predator.

Semi-Aquatic 1 Film Appearance

Vital Statistics

Length50 ft (15.2 m)
Height20 ft (6 m) at sail
Weight12–20 tons
Bite Force4,000 PSI
Top Speed15 mph (in water)
Lifespan20–25 years
DietPiscivore / Carnivore

Appearances

2001 Jurassic Park III — The main antagonist. Kills a T-Rex, attacks the plane, and pursues the survivors throughout Isla Sorna.
— No other film appearances in the original trilogy.
— Later appeared in Jurassic World: Camp Cretaceous and Jurassic World: Dominion.
  • Sound Design: The Spinosaurus roar was created from a mix of bear, lion, and bird sounds to make it feel unique and terrifying.
  • Distinctive Call: Its call was designed to be deeper and more guttural than the T-Rex, emphasizing its size.
  • Water Sounds: Splashing and water movements were added to reflect its semi-aquatic nature.
  • Silence: The Spinosaurus is often silent before attacking — making its roar even more shocking.
  • Spinosaurus is the largest carnivorous dinosaur to appear in the Jurassic Park films.
  • Its fight with the T-Rex in JP3 is one of the most controversial scenes in the franchise.
  • Real Spinosaurus was semi-aquatic — the film was ahead of its time in portraying this.
  • The sail was used for display, thermoregulation, and possibly swimming.
  • The Spinosaurus was created with a mix of CGI and animatronics.
