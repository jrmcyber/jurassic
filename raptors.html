Velociraptor

Apex Predator

Field Report

The Velociraptor is the most intelligent dinosaur in the Jurassic Park series. They are pack hunters, capable of coordinated attacks and problem-solving. Their cunning and speed make them one of the deadliest predators on Isla Nublar and Isla Sorna.

In the original film, they are shown as highly strategic — flanking their prey, testing fences, and even learning how to open doors. Robert Muldoon's final words, "Clever girl," perfectly capture their terrifying intelligence.

Appearances

1993 Jurassic Park — The kitchen scene, the "clever girl" moment, and the final battle in the visitor center.
1997 The Lost World — Tall grass attack, hunting the team on Isla Sorna.
2001 Jurassic Park III — Feathered quills, ambush in the laboratory ruins, and communication with the main pack.
  • Vocalizations: The raptor sounds were created from a mix of horse screams, dolphin squeaks, and bird calls.
  • Communication: The clicking and chattering sounds were inspired by birds and reptiles.
  • Breathing: Heavy breathing was added to create tension during the kitchen scene.
  • The Raptor Call: The iconic "raptor call" sound was designed to feel like a warning between pack members.
  • The film raptors are based on Deinonychus, not actual Velociraptors. Real ones were turkey-sized.
  • In Jurassic Park III, raptors were given feather-like quills, reflecting newer scientific discoveries.
  • The kitchen scene was inspired by a nightmare the screenwriter had.
  • "Clever girl" is one of the most quoted lines in cinema history.
  • The raptors in the film are roughly 6 feet tall — much larger than their real-life counterparts.
